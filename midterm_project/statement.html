Design Statement

Color palette on top. Rationale below. The tone follows the calm rhythm of the Faron Region.

Concept and Rationale

I chose The Legend of Zelda because the Faron Region holds calm power. The jungles feel alive with rain and light. The rivers feel steady and deep. The ancient ruins invite quiet reflection. Many scenes feel eerie at first glance. I see beauty that asks for patience and time.

I focused on the southeast coast of Hyrule. These places remind me of travel in Vietnam. I remember beaches, warm air, and dense green trees. I think about family visits and long walks near the water. I have not returned in many years. This project helps me remember those moments with care.

Ambience and Experience Goals

The site aims to slow the visitor. The text uses simple rhythm. The images sit on scroll panels that feel like field notes. The layout leaves generous space for breath. Each page presents a clear path. The goal is calm discovery and easy reading.

Color Choices

The palette builds from jungle greens, deep blues, and pale parchment. Greens suggest leaves, moss, and soft light under trees. Blues suggest rivers, storms, and the evening sky. Pale parchment suggests maps and journals. The combination supports adventure with quiet tone. Accents add warmth without noise.

Type and Hierarchy

Headings use a Zelda inspired display face to evoke legend and age. Body text uses PT Sans for clarity and comfort. Titles stay bold and steady. Paragraphs keep moderate line length. Buttons use clear labels and simple shapes. The structure guides the eye with ease.

Assets and Imagery

Images highlight Lurelin Village, Lakeside Stable, Floria Bridge, and the Zonai Ruins. Each image sits on a scroll container styled in CSS. Captions remain minimal for focus. Alt text supports accessibility. The design avoids visual clutter and keeps attention on the place.
