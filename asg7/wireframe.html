Advantages and Disadvantages of Sketching Wireframes

Sketching wireframes by hand makes it easier to explore ideas quickly and stay creative during the early stages of a design. It allows you to experiment with different layouts and make changes freely without worrying about technical details. This process also encourages teamwork since it is simple to share ideas on paper. However, hand sketches are not always accurate or consistent, and it can be difficult to update or share them later. Using wireframe software provides cleaner layouts, better organization, and easier collaboration, but it may limit creativity compared to sketching by hand.

Best Wireframing Style for My Project

For my AR Science Learning Project, a combination of sketching and digital wireframing works best. Starting with quick sketches helps me visualize my ideas and plan how each section, such as the AR-3D Model page and the Lesson page, will look. Once the layout feels clear and organized, moving to a digital wireframe helps keep the design consistent and ready for development. Since my project involves interactive features like 3D models and learning cards, using both styles balances creativity with structure and makes the site easy to navigate and understand.

Reflection

Personally, I enjoy creating wireframes because they help me clearly visualize my design style and plan what elements to include in my HTML layout. When I start a project, I often have ideas for how I want it to look, but jumping straight into coding can make me feel stuck. Wireframing gives me the chance to organize my thoughts and map out the structure before I touch the code. It also helps me see how each section connects and flows on the page, which saves time later when building the site.
