Spider-Man

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse

Overview

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse is a 2018 American computer-animated superhero film featuring the Marvel Comics character Spider-Man. Produced by Sony Pictures Animation and released by Columbia Pictures in association with Marvel, the movie introduced audiences to the multiverse concept with multiple versions of Spider-Man. The film follows teenager Miles Morales as he becomes Spider-Man and joins other Spider-People from alternate dimensions to stop Kingpin and save New York City.

The film was praised for its unique animation style, blending 3D computer animation with traditional comic-book techniques like halftones and motion lines. It received widespread acclaim for its story, humor, and representation of diverse Spider-heroes, including Gwen Stacy, Spider-Man Noir, Peni Parker, and Spider-Ham. The movie won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature, making it the first non-Disney or Pixar film to win the category since 2011.

The soundtrack played a huge role in the movie’s success, with “Sunflower” by Post Malone and Swae Lee becoming the breakout hit. The song not only topped charts but also became a cultural anthem tied to Miles Morales’ journey. Its popularity helped the film connect with both younger audiences and older fans of Spider-Man.
